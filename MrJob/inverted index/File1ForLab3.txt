18	It is a truth universally acknowledged, that a single man in possession
19	of a good fortune, must be in want of a wife.
20	
21	However little known the feelings or views of such a man may be on his
22	first entering a neighbourhood, this truth is so well fixed in the minds
23	of the surrounding families, that he is considered the rightful property
24	of some one or other of their daughters.
25	
26	“My dear Mr. Bennet,” said his lady to him one day, “have you heard that
27	Netherfield Park is let at last?”
28	
29	Mr. Bennet replied that he had not.
30	
31	“But it is,” returned she; “for Mrs. Long has just been here, and she
32	told me all about it.”
33	
34	Mr. Bennet made no answer.
